September 15th 2025

The "Scaling Up Podcast" interviews Elias Lopez "Doomsday"

Professional Wrestler Elias Lopez "Doomsday" makes an appearance on the "Scaling Up Podcast" on a platform called Youtube. The hosts of this podcast are Eric Kanevsky and Tanner (Unknown Last Name). The video is 31 minutes and 57 seconds long.

Here are some key moments from the interview:

Elias claims that he was only told about Raja's involvement during match 1, when his match was match 4. That every wrestler in the match was against the idea of Raja being in the match. That Raja was only supposed to come up to his manager, distract the ref and give Stu a single punch in the face.

Erik questions Elias on Timmy pinning Stuart after the assault on Stuart had concluded. Elias states that Timmy was trying to "snap the ref back the reality" and that he shielded Stuart's body as well as rolling him to the side of the ring to prevent Stuart from choking on his own blood. Elias states that he was struggling to restrain Raja as he had injured his arm earlier in the professional wrestling match.

Elias retells a story on of 2 seperate training incidents with KnokXPro Entertainment & Academy, when he was 16 years old. The first of which he got a minor concussion after failing a bump (a fall) from a clothes line (professional wrestling move). The doctor told him to take take 1-2 weeks rest, upon his return to training, he hit his head again after being DDT'd (professional wrestling move) which caused him to have a brain bleed and 2 seizures. During this discussion, Erik (former professional wrestler at KnoksXPro Entertainment & Academy) and Elias both agreed that the ring is too hard and that they should've replaced it a long time ago, Erik states that it is the same ring as they had 11 years ago. Elias states the mentality is that "if you can do it on this mat, you can do it anywhere". Erik confirms this sentiment by stating he was told by an old school wrestler there that said they used to train on concrete.

Erik then retells a story of an incident that happened in a practice match at KnokXPro Entertainment & Academy, he states he wasn't taught a technique to mitigate damage when someone jumps on you, someone jumped on him with their knees into his spleen, his injury was a ruptured spleen causing internal bleeding, he almost died because of this. He recalls that his mum told the trainers about his near death experience and coach says that Erik needs to work on his selling (making hits seem believable). Erik states he was told by doctors to avoid wrestling training for 1 year, 4 months into his recovery, he heard news that a wrestler died at KnokXPro Entertainment & Academy but doesn't have any details of the death, the death may not have been wrestling related. Erik states that during this wrestlers funeral, some of the trainers were taking selfies smiling whilst holding his casket and posting them and shortly after posted videos moving of general professional wrestling promotion. After seeing this, Erik left the organisation.

Erik: "Both of our situations could've been prevented with better training."

Elias claims that he has spoken to Stuart and his family wouldn't divuldge any further information for privacy reasons.
