Raja Jackson Vs Syko Stu: A Complete Timeline (Page 1 of 3)

This timeline is based on publicly available livestream footage, media reports, and interviews. Some details remain unverified and are noted as such.

Quick Brief

Raja Jackson is the son of MMA superstar Quinton "Rampage" Jackson. [1] The following is a timeline of events leading up to, during and following his alledged assault on professional wrestler (and retired US Army veteran) Stuart Smith (Aka. Syko Stu).

23rd August 2025:

Around 02:05 UTC (19:05 Local Time)

Raja Starts his Livestream

Raja starts his livestream on a platform called KICK on an account called RAJAPLUS. [2] Raja walks towards 8548 Lankershim Blvd, Sun Valley, California where a professional wrestling event called KnokXperience [3] is taking place held by an organisation called KnokX Pro Entertainment & Academy.

KnokX Pro Entertainment & Academy is a professional wrestling organization and training school deeply rooted in the legacy of the Anoaʻi family [4] ,a renowned dynasty in professional wrestling. [5] KnokX Pro Entertainment & Academy was established around 2009. [6]

Raja Mentions his Potential Concussion

Raja: (responding to a livestream comment) How does my head feel? I'm still fucked up" [7]

Raja is referencing a reported concussion he was the receipient of, in a mixed martial arts sparring session on 21/08/2025 where he was kicked in the head. [8] This incident happened at an mma gym called Team Body Shop. [9][10] The sparring partner is likely Lucas Brown, an amateur mixed martial arts fighter with a 5-3 record. [11]

Raja Mentions his Reason for Attending the Event

Raja: "My, err, coach, my lifting coach and shit, one of my teammates, er, Andre, who was on the stream the last time, is having a pro wrestling show and I told him, i'll erm, i'll erm, you know, I'll come and support." [7]

Raja is referring to professional wrestler Andre Joel Hudson (aka "AJ Mana" and "The Marine") who at the time of writing this article has a 3-3 mixed martial arts amateur record, he competed between 2017 - 2022. [12] Andre has been a professional wrestler since 2021. [13] He also reports to have served in the United States Marine Corps. [17]
